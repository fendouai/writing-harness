# Writing Rubric

Score each dimension from 1 to 5.

## 1. Clarity of Purpose

- `1`: The piece does not seem to know what it is trying to do.
- `3`: The general purpose is clear, but the writing wanders.
- `5`: The objective is obvious and the piece stays aligned to it.

## 2. Audience Specificity

- `1`: Could be written for anyone.
- `3`: Some audience fit is visible, but still broad.
- `5`: Clearly written for a specific reader with specific needs.

## 3. Point of View

- `1`: No meaningful stance or priority.
- `3`: Some judgment appears, but weakly.
- `5`: Clear, credible, and well-placed editorial judgment.

## 4. Specificity and Concreteness

- `1`: Mostly abstractions and broad claims.
- `3`: Some useful details, but still generalized.
- `5`: Rich with relevant specifics, constraints, or examples.

## 5. Insight Density

- `1`: Familiar phrasing with little value.
- `3`: Some useful framing, but not much beyond common sense.
- `5`: Multiple observations or distinctions that genuinely sharpen understanding.

## 6. Structural Focus

- `1`: Over-complete, repetitive, or shapeless.
- `3`: Competent structure but too even or padded.
- `5`: Strong emphasis, clear pacing, and useful organization.

## 7. Voice Credibility

- `1`: Sounds assembled, stiff, or fake.
- `3`: Mostly natural but still smoothed over.
- `5`: Sounds like a believable author speaking with intent.

## 8. Reader Usefulness

- `1`: Plausible language, low practical value.
- `3`: Helpful in parts, but not decisive.
- `5`: Helps the reader act, decide, or understand better.

## 9. Trustworthiness

- `1`: Overconfident, vague, or unsupported.
- `3`: Generally careful but not clearly grounded.
- `5`: Calibrated, credible, and appropriately bounded.

## 10. Memorability

- `1`: Nothing sticks.
- `3`: Some decent lines, but little residue.
- `5`: Contains phrasing or observations likely to stay with the reader.

## Quick Meta Checks

Ask these after scoring:

- Could this paragraph appear in many similar articles unchanged?
- Does it feel like fluent text or actual authorship?
- Is there at least one sentence worth keeping on purpose?
