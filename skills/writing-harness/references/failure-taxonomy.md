# Failure Taxonomy

Use these tags to diagnose why a draft feels AI-generated or low-authorship.

## `generic_abstraction`

The text relies on broad language that could fit many topics.

Common signs:

- heavy use of value, impact, quality, trust, growth, efficiency
- no named actors, constraints, or situations

Fix:

- replace abstraction with specifics

## `overexplained`

The draft spends too much time preparing, restating, or summarizing the point.

Common signs:

- long setup before the real claim
- redundant transitions
- summary sentences that add nothing

Fix:

- cut throat-clearing and compress repeats

## `no_real_thesis`

The piece has information but no strong center.

Common signs:

- many reasonable points, no priority
- conclusion does not sharpen the piece

Fix:

- identify the one thing the piece is actually trying to say

## `false_balance`

The writing hides behind fairness instead of making a judgment.

Common signs:

- "it depends" without a decision rule
- all options presented as equally valid

Fix:

- state what matters more and under what conditions

## `stock_phrase`

The language sounds borrowed from generic AI or business writing.

Common signs:

- "it is important to note"
- "in today's fast-paced world"
- "this helps improve efficiency"

Fix:

- rewrite the sentence around the actual point

## `weak_example`

The draft claims something useful but does not ground it.

Common signs:

- examples are too broad or too polished
- assertions are left floating

Fix:

- add a concrete scenario, tradeoff, or consequence

## `empty_empathy`

The text performs warmth or concern without insight.

Common signs:

- generic reassurance
- tone says "I care" but content does not prove it

Fix:

- replace performed tone with practical understanding

## `uniform_rhythm`

Every sentence or paragraph feels evenly shaped.

Common signs:

- repetitive sentence length
- balanced paragraph blocks
- no emphasis shifts

Fix:

- vary pacing and give unequal space to unequal ideas

## `insightless_summary`

The draft ends by restating instead of advancing.

Common signs:

- clean conclusion with no added value
- summary language that sounds complete but empty

Fix:

- end on a sharper claim, implication, or decision rule

## `speaker_invisible`

The reader cannot sense who is talking.

Common signs:

- no perspective
- no preference
- no implied experience or editorial stance

Fix:

- make the author visible through judgment, emphasis, and selection
